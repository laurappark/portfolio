About Rainbows for All Children

Client: Rainbows for All Children, a non-profit serving children aged 3-18 who are navigating grief after a loss of a parent or guardian.

Overview: As a part of Design and Innovate for Social Change on campus, I was a part of a team of 4 that worked to develop a mobile application offering diverse activities and features tailored to children navigating grief and loss.

My Role: I took on most of the UX/UI design work. Beginning with user research, I learned about our users' pain points and client goals to design an empathetic healing experience. From this, I sketched ideas, built wireframes, and designed a final prototype.

DESIGN PROCESS

PROBLEM

Meeting the diverse needs of users aged 7-18.

FOCUS

Striking a balance between familiarity and surprise, thus encouraging users to explore the app extensively without feeling the urge to exit prematurely.

SOLUTION

Interactive elements by blending vibrant visuals with intuitive navigation and cues that guide users. through the app

RESULT

70% of users expressed that they felt more of a sense of accomplishment with clear direction resulting in reduced abandonment.

PROBLEM

Maintaining user confidentiality and data privacy.

FOCUS

Privacy-conscious but inclusive approach through Firebase, the real-time database integrated with the app that minimizes data stored.

SOLUTION

All users, whether signed in or not, can engage in the journaling process. Sign in is prompted only if they choose to save their entry which then adds data into Firebase.

RESULT

Non-profitâ€™s leadership felt that this bifurcated system "strikes a balance between accessibility and data protection", preserving user privacy while still fostering an open environment for self-expression and healing.

PROBLEM

Consider and avoid potential triggers from past thoughts and emotions.

FOCUS

Uphold the app's commitment to fostering a nurturing healing journey allowing users to be able to reflect on past emotions while having autonomy over their journey.

SOLUTION

'Delete Entries' button on the Profile Page, enabling users to remove entries that they find triggering or distressing. The corresponding entry is then immediately deleted from Firebase.

RESULT

80% of users indicated they are more likely to save journals entries if there is an option to delete it in the future.

TAKEAWAYS

CONNECT WITH USERS

Being able to meet directly with users to hear their feeback equipped me with the ability to prioritize user well-being and create an emotionally supportive experience. I realized the power of building a connection with the users you are building the product for.

COMMUNICATE EARLY & OFTEN

When faced with constraints or challenges that impacted the project's scope or timeline, it worked best to prioritize clear, timely, and transparent communication with the client. This ensured that there were no surprises and that we could collectively find solutions.

RESEARCH

Methods for data privacy was something I had no prior knowledge in. Taking a proactive approach to research allowed me to identify alternative solutions or adjustments that aligned with the project's goal.
